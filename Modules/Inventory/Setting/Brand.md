A **Brand** represents a product label or manufacturer identity used to categorize items within the inventory.
Brands help companies manage product lines, analyze performance, and provide better organization within catalogs, stock reports, and sales workflows.

Brand is especially useful for **retail, distribution, manufacturing, electronics, FMCG**, and any business dealing with multi-brand inventory.

---

# **1. Purpose of Brand Management**

Brand management within the system helps:

* Categorize items by brand for better identification.
* Improve search and filtering for sales, warehouse, and catalog operations.
* Analyze brand-wise sales, demand, and stock distribution.
* Create clear reporting structures.
* Support marketing, merchandising, and reordering strategies.
* Group items under a recognized market identity.

Branding brings clarity to product catalogs and helps in strategic business decision-making.

# **4. Brand in Item Master**

Each item can be assigned a brand. This helps:

* Identify items quickly.
* Filter items by brand in reports.
* Use brand-specific procurement strategies.
* Manage pricing or marketing based on brand.

**Note:** Item belongs to only one brand, but a brand can have multiple items.

---

# **5. Operational Usage of Brand**

### **5.1 In Sales Operations**

* Sales teams can filter by brand in item selectors.
* Brand-level promotions or pricing strategies can be implemented.

### **5.2 In Purchase Operations**

* Procure items from preferred suppliers of a specific brand.
* Evaluate vendor performance by brand.

### **5.3 In Warehouse Management**

* Group items by brand for storage planning.
* Quick identification during picking or cycle counts.

### **5.4 In Reporting**

Brand-based reports improve planning, forecasting, and inventory analysis.
